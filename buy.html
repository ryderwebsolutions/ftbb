Built specifically for the Irish property market — not adapted from a British or American template. Every chapter covers the exact schemes, rules, legal processes, and pitfalls that apply when buying in Ireland.

Whether you're just starting to think about buying or you're already in the process, the guide moves with you — giving you what you need at each stage.

Why the FTBB Guide

What Sets It Apart

Clear, Not Complicated

The Irish buying process is confusing by default. The FTBB guide cuts through the jargon and explains every step in plain English — so you know exactly what's happening and what to do next.

Built for the Irish Market

Central Bank rules, Help to Buy, First Home Scheme, Revenue mortgage credit, solicitor costs, stamp duty — everything is specific to buying property in the Republic of Ireland, not imported from the UK or elsewhere.

Avoid Costly Mistakes

First-time buyers lose time and money on preventable errors — poor solicitor choice, missed grants, weak mortgage applications. The guide covers the most common mistakes in detail so you don't repeat them.

Used Alongside Your Advisors

The guide doesn't replace your mortgage broker or solicitor. It prepares you for every conversation — so you ask the right questions and understand the answers without relying on others to explain everything from scratch.

Up to Date for 2026

Irish mortgage rules, grant scheme limits, and government support programmes change regularly. The FTBB guide reflects the current rules as of 2026, including the latest Central Bank lending limits and Help to Buy changes.

Checklists for Every Stage

Each chapter includes practical checklists so you know exactly what to do, what to have ready, and what to ask — from your first mortgage inquiry to the day you collect your keys.

Who It's For

Written for Anyone Serious About Buying Their First Home in Ireland

You don't need to be a financial expert or know the buying process already. The FTBB guide starts from the beginning — and brings you all the way through to moving in.

  • You've decided to buy and want to understand the full process before you start
  • You're already saving and want to know exactly how much you need and when you'll be ready
  • You've been approved in principle but find the legal process confusing
  • You want to make sure you're not missing out on any grants or schemes
  • You've found a property and need to understand what happens next
  • You've had a setback and want to approach it differently this time
