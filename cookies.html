Cookie Policy

Last updated: 1 June 2025

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how FTBB (ftbb.ie) uses them, and how you can manage your preferences. When you visit our website, we may place cookies on your device.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to remember your preferences and to provide information to website owners. Cookies cannot run programmes or deliver viruses to your device.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential functionality — to remember that you have accepted our cookie notice, so we do not show it on every page visit.
  • Analytics — to understand how visitors use ftbb.ie so we can improve our content and user experience. We use Google Analytics for this purpose.

We do not use cookies to serve targeted advertising or to track your browsing activity across other websites.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can withdraw your consent and reset your cookie preferences at any time using the button below. You can also control cookies through your browser settings — most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Please note that disabling cookies may affect certain functionality on this website.

Reset your cookie preferences — this will clear your saved consent and show the cookie notice again on your next visit.

For instructions on how to manage cookies in your browser, visit:

  • Google Chrome: support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
  • Mozilla Firefox: support.mozilla.org
  • Safari: support.apple.com
  • Microsoft Edge: support.microsoft.com

Third-Party Cookies

Google Analytics cookies are set by Google Ireland Limited. FTBB uses these tools to measure website traffic and improve the user experience. Google may use this data in accordance with their own privacy policy. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in legislation, technology or our practices. Updated versions will be posted on this page with a revised "Last updated" date. We recommend reviewing this page periodically.
