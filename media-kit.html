Editorially Independent, Reader-First

FTBB — First Time Buyer's Bible — is Ireland's most comprehensive resource platform for people navigating their first property purchase. We don't sell mortgages, push products, or take referral fees from lenders. We connect buyers with the right expertise at the right moment in their journey.

Our content covers every stage of the Irish buying process: from understanding Central Bank lending limits and the Help to Buy scheme, to navigating solicitor contracts, choosing a surveyor, and managing the mortgage drawdown.

Businesses that advertise with FTBB gain access to our audience in a context of trust and expert guidance — which is the most effective position from which to reach first-time buyers.

  • Published independently — not affiliated with any lender or estate agent
  • Editorially led — content is written to inform, not to sell
  • Magazine and digital publication with nationwide reach
  • Active social media presence on Instagram: @first_timebuyerire

Audience Overview

Who Reads FTBB

High-Intent Readers in Active Purchase Mode

FTBB readers are Irish adults actively preparing to buy their first home. They are in a high-intent research phase — comparing mortgage providers, evaluating solicitors, planning home purchases, and making spending decisions across a wide range of categories simultaneously.

They are not passive readers. They come to FTBB with specific questions and use our guides as a reference throughout their buying journey — which often lasts 12–24 months from initial research to purchase completion.

Key insight: First-time buyers in Ireland are making many of their most significant consumer decisions — mortgage provider, solicitor, insurer, furniture, kitchen, flooring — all within a concentrated 6–18 month window. The businesses that reach them during this period establish long-term customer relationships.

Advertiser Fit

Who Should Advertise With FTBB

Any business that serves first-time buyers at any point in their purchase journey — from the decision to buy through to home ownership — is a natural fit for FTBB advertising.

Finance

Mortgage Brokers & Financial Advisors

Buyers actively comparing lenders and seeking mortgage guidance before choosing a broker. High-intent at the point of reading.

Property

Estate Agents & Property Developers

Buyers searching for their first property across all Irish counties, including new developments eligible for Help to Buy.

Legal

Conveyancing Solicitors

Buyers selecting legal representation for the most significant transaction of their lives. Solicitor selection is a key FTBB guide topic.

Professional Services

Surveyors & Structural Engineers

Buyers who need building surveys before proceeding with purchase — guided specifically by FTBB content on surveys.

Insurance

Buyers comparing home insurance and mortgage protection before drawdown — a mandatory step in the buying process.

Home & Living

Furniture, Kitchens & Home Suppliers

First-time buyers furnishing a home from scratch — often making significant first-time purchases across all home categories.

Why FTBB

Why Advertise With FTBB

Contextual, Not Interruptive

FTBB advertising appears within expert guides that buyers are actively reading — not as pop-ups or irrelevant display ads. Your brand appears when readers are in the exact mental state to consider your services.

High-Intent Audience

FTBB readers are not casual browsers. They are people preparing to make one of the largest financial decisions of their lives. The purchase intent, research depth, and spending readiness of this audience is exceptionally high.

Trusted Editorial Environment

FTBB is read as a trusted, independent resource — not a commercial lead-generation site. Advertising within a trusted editorial context carries significantly more weight than generic digital advertising.

National Reach, Local Relevance

FTBB reaches buyers across all 32 counties of Ireland. Whether your business serves a specific region or operates nationally, our audience includes buyers searching in every part of the country.
